I've analyzed the peacock motif. It's a classic Kanjivaram Mayura pattern. Here's my recommendation:

• Body weave: Twill 2/2 — gives the silk its characteristic sheen
• Motif structure: Compound satin (8-harness) for the peacock plumage — allows the fine feather details
• Tail feathers: Float length 5–7 for maximum lustre effect

BK
What's the difference between Kanjivaram and Banarasi weave structures?
✦
Great question! Key differences:

Kanjivaram (Tamil Nadu):
· Pure mulberry silk warp & weft
· Body, border, pallu woven separately then joined (korvai technique)
· Zari typically pure silver/gold-coated thread
· Hook count: 1800–2400 standard

Banarasi (Varanasi):
· Silk warp + heavier brocade weft
· Mughal-inspired florals, tanchoi & cutwork structures
· Extravagant zari floating on surface
· Hook count: 2400–3200 for complex motifs

SJ-DAS has style-specific templates for both. Want me to load the Banarasi weave library?
